中文 EN

Professor Jinkai Wang is a faculty member recruited by Sun Yat-sen University and is accredited as a doctoral supervisor. He obtained his Bachelor of Science degree from the School of Life Sciences at Wuhan University in 2005. In 2010, he earned his Ph.D. in Genetics from the Kunming Institute of Zoology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the supervision of Researcher Bing Su. From 2010 to 2016, he conducted postdoctoral research in bioinformatics in Professor Yi Xing’s laboratory at the University of Iowa (2010–2012) and the University of California, Los Angeles (2013–2016). In 2016, he was appointed as a talent recruit by Sun Yat-sen University. In 2018, he was selected for the “Young Top-Notch Talent” program under the Guangdong Province Pearl River Talent Plan.

Welcome to Our Lab!

Since its establishment, our lab has published 13 research articles in prestigious academic journals such as Molecular Cell (2 papers), Nature Chemical Biology, Nature Communications (2 papers), and Nucleic Acids Research (2 papers). With the exception of two review articles, each of the remaining 11 papers has an impact factor (IF) higher than 10.

The lab’s primary research focus lies in leveraging second- and third-generation high-throughput sequencing technologies, combined with bioinformatics, genomics, and molecular biology approaches, to develop detection techniques and algorithms for m6A RNA modification. We investigate the regulatory mechanisms and patterns of m6A RNA modification, as well as its roles in critical physiological and pathological processes such as stem cell growth and tumor progression.

Employing a "dry and wet lab integrated" strategy, the laboratory conducts fundamental medical research by developing computational algorithms, experimental techniques, and omics technologies. This allows us to decipher RNA modification patterns from novel dimensions, propose cutting-edge scientific questions from fresh perspectives, and rigorously elucidate these questions through biochemical experiments and omics analysis.
